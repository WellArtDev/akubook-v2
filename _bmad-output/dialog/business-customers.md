## Ideal Business Customer Profile

### Company Characteristics

**Size:**
- **Employees:** 100+ (mid-size company)
- **Revenue:** Rp 1M+ per month
- **Stage:** Established business (not startup)

**Industry Focus:**
- **Primary:** Distributors (electronics, sound systems, industrial supplies, etc.)
- **Secondary:** Retail chains, manufacturing, service companies
- **Common trait:** Multi-location operations, complex inventory needs

**Tech Maturity:**
- Already using business software (Accurate, Jurnal, etc.)
- Not manual/Excel-only operations
- Ready for upgrade/consolidation
- Comfortable with software adoption

**Geographic:**
- Indonesia (primary market)
- Urban and semi-urban areas
- Multi-branch/warehouse operations

## Current State & Pain Points

### Current Tools
- **Accounting:** Accurate (offline or online)
- **Attendance:** Separate online attendance app (subscription)
- **HRM:** Mix of software + manual/Excel
- **Pain:** Multiple disconnected tools, subscription fatigue

### Key Pain Points (Priority Order)
1. **Tool sprawl:** Multiple logins, data not integrated
2. **Subscription cost:** Monthly fees adding up (especially attendance apps)
3. **Data control:** Want on-premise option for sensitive data
4. **Migration complexity:** Fear of losing historical data

### Trigger Events
- Monthly bill review → "We're paying how much for separate tools?"
- Audit/compliance need → "We need data on-premise"
- Growth pain → "Current tools don't scale with multi-warehouse"
- Contract renewal → "Should we switch before renewing?"

## Decision-Making Structure

### Buying Roles

**Decision Maker:**
- **Role:** Director / Owner
- **Authority:** Final budget approval
- **Concerns:** ROI, total cost of ownership, business continuity
- **Decision criteria:** Cost savings, operational efficiency, risk mitigation

**Influencer/Evaluator:**
- **Role:** HRD Manager / Operations Manager / IT Manager
- **Authority:** Recommends solution, conducts evaluation
- **Concerns:** Operational fit, ease of use, migration effort
- **Decision criteria:** Feature completeness, user experience, support quality

**End Users:**
- **Roles:** Finance Admin, Sales, Purchasing, Warehouse, Staff
- **Authority:** None (but feedback influences evaluator)
- **Concerns:** Ease of use, training curve, daily workflow
- **Decision criteria:** Intuitive interface, mobile access, reliability

### Decision Process

**Phase 1: Discovery (1-2 weeks)**
- Evaluator researches alternatives
- Initial contact with vendors
- High-level feature comparison

**Phase 2: Evaluation (2-4 weeks)**
- Demo/trial period
- Test with real data
- User feedback collection
- Migration feasibility assessment

**Phase 3: Approval (1-2 weeks)**
- Evaluator presents recommendation to Director
- Director reviews ROI and risks
- Budget approval and contract negotiation

**Total cycle:** 1-3 months (not impulse buy)

## Buyer Personas

### Persona 1: The Pragmatic Director

**Profile:**
- Age: 40-55
- Role: Director / Owner
- Experience: 10-20 years in business
- Tech comfort: Moderate (delegates to team)

**Goals:**
- Reduce operational costs
- Improve business visibility
- Scale operations efficiently
- Maintain data control

**Pain Points:**
- Subscription costs eating into margins
- Can't get consolidated reports across systems
- Worried about vendor lock-in
- Needs ROI justification for board/investors

**Decision Criteria:**
- Total cost of ownership (5-year view)
- Business continuity risk
- Vendor stability and support
- Migration effort and downtime

**Messaging:**
- "One-time payment = predictable costs"
- "All-in-one = no more tool sprawl"
- "On-premise = you own your data"
- "Proven migration process = minimal disruption"

### Persona 2: The Operational Manager

**Profile:**
- Age: 30-45
- Role: HRD / Operations / IT Manager
- Experience: 5-15 years
- Tech comfort: High (hands-on evaluation)

**Goals:**
- Streamline daily operations
- Reduce manual work and errors
- Improve team productivity
- Get better reporting and insights

**Pain Points:**
- Switching between multiple tools daily
- Manual data entry and reconciliation
- Training staff on different systems
- Support tickets across multiple vendors

**Decision Criteria:**
- Feature completeness (does it do everything we need?)
- Ease of use (will staff adopt it?)
- Migration support (can we move our data?)
- Support quality (will we get help when stuck?)

**Messaging:**
- "All modules in one dashboard"
- "Auto-configuration by industry"
- "Import data from Accurate/Jurnal"
- "1 month included support + flexible paid options"

## Sales Strategy Implications

### Demo Approach
- **Industry-specific setup:** Show distributor workflow (PO → SO → surat jalan → accounting)
- **Data migration:** Demonstrate import from Accurate
- **Module flexibility:** Show enable/disable in real-time
- **Cost comparison:** One-time vs 5-year subscription cost

### Trial Period
- **Duration:** 30 days (enough for real evaluation)
- **Scope:** Full features, real data import
- **Support:** Dedicated onboarding assistance
- **Success criteria:** Key workflows tested, user feedback positive

### Objection Handling

**"One-time payment = no updates?"**
- Lifetime updates and bug fixes included
- Show update history and roadmap

**"What if we outgrow it?"**
- Unlimited users, multi-company support
- Modular system scales with business

**"Migration is too risky"**
- Proven migration process from Accurate/Jurnal
- Parallel run option (test before full switch)
- Dedicated migration support

**"What about support?"**
- 1 month included, then flexible options
- Per-incident or monthly retainer (customer chooses)
- Priority support available

# Tone of Voice: AkuBook

**Date:** 2026-05-12

---

## Tone of Voice Overview

AkuBook's tone of voice reflects its positioning as a **professional yet approachable** B2B ERP for Indonesian SMEs. The tone balances trustworthiness (critical for financial software) with ease-of-use (delivering on "lebih enteng dari Accurate").

---

## Tone Attributes

### 1. Professional tapi Approachable

**Why:** B2B ERP handling financial data requires trust and credibility, but AkuBook differentiates by being less intimidating than Accurate. Professional enough for Finance Managers, approachable enough for first-time ERP users.

**In practice:**
- Use proper Bahasa Indonesia (not overly casual)
- Avoid corporate jargon and bureaucratic language
- Speak to users as colleagues, not subordinates

### 2. Clear & Direct

**Why:** Users work with critical financial data. Ambiguity causes errors, confusion, and lost trust. Clear instructions and direct feedback are essential.

**In practice:**
- Short, specific instructions
- No flowery language or unnecessary words
- Error messages explain what's wrong and how to fix it
- Button labels describe the action clearly

### 3. Helpful & Supportive

**Why:** Many users are transitioning from Accurate or manual systems. They need guidance, not intimidation. Helpful tone reduces learning curve and builds confidence.

**In practice:**
- Provide context and hints where needed
- Anticipate user questions with tooltips
- Celebrate successes (but not excessively)
- Frame errors as fixable problems, not user failures

### 4. Indonesian-Friendly

**Why:** Target market is Indonesian SMEs. Natural Bahasa Indonesia feels familiar and trustworthy. Awkward translations or unnecessary English create distance.

**In practice:**
- Use natural Indonesian phrasing (not literal English translations)
- Consistent terminology (don't switch between "Simpan" and "Save")
- Use English only for universal tech terms (e.g., "Dashboard", "Email")
- Avoid mixing languages unnecessarily

## Do's and Don'ts

### ✅ Do's

**Language:**
- Use natural Bahasa Indonesia (not literal translations)
- Keep sentences short and direct
- Use active voice ("Simpan data" not "Data akan disimpan")
- Be consistent with terminology across the app

**Tone:**
- Be helpful and supportive (guide users)
- Be clear about what's happening (no ambiguity)
- Be respectful of user's time (concise messages)
- Be professional but not stiff

**User Context:**
- Anticipate user questions (provide context)
- Explain errors clearly (what's wrong + how to fix)
- Celebrate successes briefly (don't overdo it)
- Frame errors as fixable problems

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Email tidak valid. Periksa format email Anda."
- ✅ "Berhasil disimpan"
- ✅ "Kolom ini wajib diisi"
- ✅ "Hapus data ini? Tindakan ini tidak dapat dibatalkan."

### ❌ Don'ts

**Language:**
- Don't use overly formal/bureaucratic language
- Don't use slang or overly casual language
- Don't mix English and Indonesian unnecessarily
- Don't use technical jargon without explanation

**Tone:**
- Don't be condescending or patronizing
- Don't be overly enthusiastic or playful
- Don't blame users for errors
- Don't be vague or ambiguous

**User Context:**
- Don't assume users know technical terms
- Don't hide important information in fine print
- Don't use humor in error messages (frustrating context)
- Don't overload users with too much text

**Examples:**
- ❌ "Kesalahan validasi input pada kolom email" (too formal)
- ❌ "Waduh emailnya salah nih" (too casual)
- ❌ "Klik button Save untuk menyimpan" (unnecessary English mix)
- ❌ "Error 422: Unprocessable Entity" (technical jargon)

## Tone by Context

### High-Stakes Contexts (Financial Operations, Data Deletion)

**Tone:** More formal, extra clear, explicit consequences

**Examples:**
- "Hapus transaksi ini? Tindakan ini tidak dapat dibatalkan dan akan mempengaruhi laporan keuangan."
- "Posting jurnal ini akan mengubah saldo akun. Pastikan data sudah benar."
- "Tutup periode ini? Setelah ditutup, transaksi tidak dapat diubah."

**Why:** Users need to understand consequences clearly. No room for ambiguity.

### Routine Operations (Daily Tasks, Viewing Data)

**Tone:** Lighter, more conversational, efficient

**Examples:**
- "Simpan"
- "Lihat detail"
- "Ekspor ke Excel"
- "Filter berdasarkan tanggal"

**Why:** Users do these tasks frequently. Keep it simple and fast.

### Error States (Something Went Wrong)

**Tone:** Helpful, not blaming, solution-oriented

**Examples:**
- "Email tidak valid. Periksa format email Anda." (not "Anda memasukkan email yang salah")
- "Tidak ada koneksi internet. Periksa koneksi Anda dan coba lagi." (not "Koneksi gagal")
- "Password salah. Coba lagi atau reset password." (not "Autentikasi gagal")

**Why:** Users are already frustrated. Help them fix the problem, don't make it worse.

### Success States (Task Completed)

**Tone:** Brief, positive, move on

**Examples:**
- "Berhasil disimpan"
- "Invoice berhasil dikirim"
- "Pembayaran tercatat"

**Why:** Acknowledge success but don't interrupt workflow with excessive celebration.

### First-Time User Experience (Onboarding, Setup)

**Tone:** More supportive, more context, more guidance

**Examples:**
- "Selamat datang di AkuBook! Mari kita setup perusahaan Anda."
- "Pilih jenis industri untuk konfigurasi otomatis modul yang relevan."
- "Anda bisa mengubah pengaturan ini kapan saja di menu Pengaturan."

**Why:** First-time users need more hand-holding. Reduce anxiety, build confidence.

## Voice vs. Tone

**Voice** = Consistent personality (Professional, Approachable, Clear, Helpful)
**Tone** = Adapts to context (Formal for high-stakes, Lighter for routine, Supportive for errors)

**Voice stays the same.** AkuBook is always professional, approachable, clear, and helpful.

**Tone adjusts.** More formal when deleting data, lighter when viewing reports, supportive when errors occur.

## Application Across Platforms

### Web Application

**Context:** Desktop users, complex workflows, data entry

**Tone application:**
- Clear labels and instructions (no ambiguity)
- Helpful tooltips for complex features
- Concise error messages with solutions
- Professional but not stiff

**Example:**
```
Form: "Buat Pesanan Penjualan"
Field: "Pelanggan" (with autocomplete)
Tooltip: "Pilih pelanggan atau buat baru"
Button: "Simpan" | "Batal"
Error: "Pelanggan wajib dipilih"
Success: "Pesanan berhasil disimpan"
```

### Mobile Application

**Context:** Staff users, simple tasks, on-the-go

**Tone application:**
- Even more concise (limited screen space)
- Action-oriented (tap, swipe, quick tasks)
- Supportive for first-time users
- Clear feedback for offline/online state

**Example:**
```
Screen: "Absensi"
Button: "Clock In"
Status: "Lokasi terdeteksi: Kantor Pusat"
Success: "Berhasil clock in"
Offline: "Tersimpan offline. Akan disinkronkan saat online."
```

### WhatsApp Notifications (Wablas)

**Context:** External communication, brief updates

**Tone application:**
- Professional but friendly (representing company)
- Clear and actionable
- Include relevant details (who, what, when)
- Provide next steps or links

**Example:**
```
"Halo [Nama],

Pengajuan cuti Anda untuk 15-17 Mei 2026 telah disetujui.

Sisa cuti: 8 hari

Lihat detail: [link]

- AkuBook"
```

## Tone of Voice Summary

**Core Attributes:**
1. **Professional tapi Approachable** — Trustworthy but not intimidating
2. **Clear & Direct** — No ambiguity, especially for data-critical operations
3. **Helpful & Supportive** — Guide users, reduce learning curve
4. **Indonesian-Friendly** — Natural Bahasa Indonesia, not awkward translations

**Key Principles:**
- Speak to users as colleagues, not subordinates
- Be clear about what's happening and what to do next
- Adapt tone to context (formal for high-stakes, lighter for routine)
- Use consistent terminology across the app

**Ultimate Goal:**
> Users should feel confident and supported, not intimidated or confused. AkuBook speaks like a helpful colleague who knows their stuff but doesn't show off.

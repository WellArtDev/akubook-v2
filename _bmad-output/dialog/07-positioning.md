# Positioning: AkuBook

**Date:** 2026-05-12

---

## Positioning Statement

Untuk small & mid-size companies di Indonesia yang lelah dengan subscription fatigue dan data scattered across multiple tools, AkuBook adalah all-in-one ERP yang menggabungkan accounting, attendance, dan HRM dalam satu platform dengan one-time payment. Berbeda dengan Accurate, Jurnal, atau Mekari yang mengunci bisnis dalam monthly fees dan cloud dependency, AkuBook memberikan ownership penuh dengan on-premise deployment, modular architecture untuk extensibility, dan data migration dari tools existing — sehingga bisnis bisa consolidate operations tanpa buang investasi atau mulai dari nol.

---

## Positioning Components

### Target Customer
**Small & mid-size companies (Indonesia)**
- Not enterprise (too complex needs)
- Not solo freelancers (too simple needs)
- Sweet spot: Companies with 10-100+ employees
- Industries: Distributor, retail, jasa, manufacturing

### Customer Need
**Consolidate scattered tools, escape subscription fatigue, control their data**
- Currently using: Accurate/Jurnal (accounting) + separate attendance app + Excel/manual (HRM)
- Pain: Multiple logins, monthly bills stacking up, data scattered, no on-premise option
- Desire: One dashboard for everything, ownership model, data sovereignty

### Category
**All-in-one ERP for SME**
- Not just "accounting software"
- Not just "HRIS"
- Comprehensive business management platform

### Key Benefit
**One dashboard for all operations with ownership model**
- Finance manager: No need to login to 3 different apps for monthly close
- Owner: Real-time visibility of attendance + financial health in one place
- Operations: Seamless flow from attendance → payroll → accounting

### Alternatives
**Accurate, Jurnal, Kerjoo, Mekari**
- All subscription-based SaaS
- Monthly fees that keep growing
- Cloud-only (no on-premise)
- Separate tools for different functions

### Differentiators

#### 1. One-Time Payment
- Buy once, own forever (core ERP)
- No monthly subscription trap
- Predictable cost structure

#### 2. On-Premise Option
- Full data control and sovereignty
- No vendor lock-in
- Compliance-friendly for sensitive data

#### 3. Modular Architecture
- Core features included in base purchase
- Optional modules for expansion (paid add-ons)
- Extend without breaking core system
- Future-proof investment

#### 4. Smooth Migration
- Data import from existing tools (Accurate, Jurnal, etc.)
- No starting from scratch
- Preserve historical data and workflows

#### 5. All-in-One Consolidation
- Accounting (full like Accurate)
- Attendance (geo/face/ZKTeco integration)
- HRM (payroll/leave/overtime)
- One login, one dashboard, one source of truth

## Strategic Rationale

### Why This Positioning Works

**Market Gap:**
- Existing players (Accurate, Jurnal, Mekari) all use subscription model
- No credible on-premise alternative for SME
- No true all-in-one solution (most are accounting-focused or HR-focused)

**Target Pain Point:**
- SMEs feel subscription fatigue acutely (every Rp 500k/month adds up)
- Data sovereignty concerns growing (especially post-pandemic)
- Tool sprawl creates operational friction

**Competitive Moat:**
- One-time payment model hard to compete against for subscription players
- Modular architecture allows sustainable business model (base + add-ons)
- On-premise option addresses compliance and control needs

**Switching Trigger:**
- Monthly bill review → "We're paying how much for 3 separate tools?"
- Audit/compliance need → "We need our data on-premise"
- Operational friction → "Why do I need 3 logins to close the month?"
